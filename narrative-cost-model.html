Narrative Cost Model v1.0

Bottom-up cost analysis of narrative production per gameplay hour

LoreWeaver Market Research | March 2026

1. Key Takeaways

  • €38,679 per gameplay hour: the fully loaded cost to produce narrative content in a first language (base + 30% social taxes + 15% overhead).
  • €184,143 per gameplay hour when scaled to 12 languages, driven by VO production, localization, and QA multiplication.
  • €7.9B per year in estimated global narrative spend across ~19,000 narrative titles producing ~170,000 gameplay hours annually.
  • LoreWeaver Architect targets €8,080/gameplay hour (33% of base cost) across 5 upstream authoring activities.
  • LoreWeaver Director targets runtime adaptation for live service titles via SaaS pricing and 5% content royalty.

2. Methodology

This model estimates cost per finished hour of gameplay for narrative design and production. Each of the 15 activities follows a four step framework: workflow description, time investment in labor hours, blended hourly wage, and resulting cost per gameplay hour. Wages reflect a blended US/UK/Germany weighting (50%/25%/25%) converted to EUR at 1 USD = 0.886 EUR (2025 annual average per X-Rates/ECB; consistent with the TAM/SAM/SOM analysis). Cross-reference: ECB reference rate on 27 March 2026 was EUR 1 = USD 1.1517 (1 USD = 0.868 EUR). Studio tier weighting: AAA 60%, AA 25%, Indie 10%, VN/IF 5%.

The blended wages above were cross-referenced against 2025 industry data. Key findings:

Role Document Rate (€/hr) Market Range (€/hr) Assessment
Story and World Building (Narrative Designer) 58 37 to 61 Aligned. GDC 2025 reports $142K avg across all game roles (~€58/hr). Senior narrative designers reach $72/hr (€61/hr) per ZipRecruiter.
Script and Dialogue Writing (Game Writer) 52 32 to 44 Slightly high. Glassdoor reports $38 to $51/hr for game writers (€32 to €44). Rate is defensible for senior writers and lead roles.

Narrative QA 33 14 to 22 High. Entry level QA averages $16 to $17/hr (€14 to €15). Rate likely reflects senior QA leads, specialized narrative testers, and studio overhead baked in.
Data Analytics 52 40 to 60 Aligned. Data analysts in gaming earn $50 to $70/hr (€43 to €60) at mid to senior level.

Overall assessment: Wage rates skew toward mid to senior blended averages, consistent with AAA-weighted (60%) modeling. The QA rate is the largest outlier; it may include team lead compensation or embedded overhead not separately broken out.

5. Annual Industry Volume

Steam released ~18,500 games in 2024, up 32% from 2023. In 2025, Steam exceeded 20,000 releases. The model estimates that 19,000 to 20,000 titles per year contain meaningful narrative content.

Tier Titles/Year Avg Gameplay Hours Total Hours
AAA and Big AA ~450 140 63,000
Premium Indie ~3,200 24 76,800

Note: "Narrative content" is defined broadly. Pure narrative genres (visual novels, walking simulators) represented only ~30 titles in 2024 on Steam. The 19,000 figure includes RPGs, action-adventure, simulation, and other genres where narrative design is a meaningful cost line.

Director (On-Device Runtime)

Director targets a different problem: dynamic narrative adaptation in live service and MMO titles. These games generate narrative content continuously rather than shipping a fixed script.

Model Component Pricing
SaaS license Per-title monthly fee
Content royalty 5% of narrative content revenue

Director does not directly reduce the 15 cost lines above. Instead, it enables narrative experiences that are currently impossible to ship at scale, such as personalized quest lines, adaptive NPC dialogue, and emergent storylines, without the per-hour production cost structure.

Key Assumptions

  • Currency conversion: 1 USD = 0.886 EUR (2025 annual average per X-Rates/ECB; ECB reference rate on 27 March 2026: EUR 1 = USD 1.1517)
  • Studio tier weighting: AAA 60%, AA 25%, Indie 10%, VN/IF 5%
  • Regional weighting: US 50%, UK 25%, Germany 25%
  • Overhead multiplier: 30% social taxes + 15% general overhead = 1.45x base cost
  • All "per language" activities multiply linearly; no economies of scale assumed
  • 12 languages represents a typical AAA localization scope (EFIGS + CJK + PT, RU, PL, AR)
  • Localization caveat: The fully loaded per-hour figure of €184,143 assumes 12-language localization applied to all titles. In practice, micro and experimental games (approximately 15,350 titles/year, or 80% of releases) typically ship in 1 to 2 languages. Applying a weighted average: AAA/AA titles (~3,650 titles at 8-12 languages) account for approximately 64% of total gameplay hours at the full localization cost, while micro/experimental titles (~15,350 titles at 1-2 languages) produce the remaining hours at approximately €39K-€52K per hour. The blended industry-wide cost per gameplay hour is therefore closer to €110K-€130K rather than €184K, and total annual narrative spend is more conservatively estimated at €5B-€6B rather than the €7.9B medium scenario. The €184K figure remains appropriate for AAA and premium AA titles, which are LoreWeaver's primary target market.
