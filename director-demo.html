Director generates NPC dialogue, manages mood and trust systems, drives dynamic quests, and adapts to player choices at runtime. First dialogue line appears in 1.5 seconds on an RTX 3060. The XSD schema validation layer ensures the LLM can never corrupt game state.

Cloud competitors achieve lower raw latency using large GPU clusters. Director trades slightly higher latency for zero per-session cost, complete data privacy, and offline capability. For RPGs, strategy, and adventure games, 1.5s TTFT is within acceptable UX range.

The Real Problem with Cloud AI: Cost Scales with Success

With cloud AI, the more copies your game sells, the more you pay. Every player generates API calls that hit the studio's bill. With Director, the cost of AI narrative is €0 regardless of how many people play.

Assume a single-player RPG with 80 hours of gameplay and ~20 NPC interactions per hour (dialogue, merchants, quests, ambient). That is 1,600 interactions per player.

The Witcher 3's total development budget was approximately €80M. Running its narrative through cloud AI would cost 24x the entire development budget. Cloud AI for narrative does not scale. On-device inference is the only economically viable approach for story-driven games.
