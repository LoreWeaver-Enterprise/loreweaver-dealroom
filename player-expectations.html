Player Expectations v2.0

What players expect from AI-driven narrative in games

Customers

Executive Summary

Player expectations have fundamentally shifted. Story, characters, and world now outrank graphics as game selection drivers. Players demand adaptive, reactive experiences but receive static, scripted ones. The gap between what players expect and what studios deliver continues to widen, creating a structural opportunity for tools that close it.

1. What Players Value Most Has Changed

Graphics no longer win. Story, world, and characters now drive game selection. Multiple large-scale surveys confirm this shift.

Source Sample Date Key Finding
Bain & Company N=5,243; 6 countries May 2025 22% cite gameplay as #1 reason for game selection; only 7% cite graphics. >30% put story, characters, or world in their top 5.
Newzoo Global Gamer Study N=73,000+; 36 markets 2024 65% say "interesting story" is important; 66% say "large open world" is important for PC/console.
Xbox Culture of Play N=1,500; US June/July 2025 68% say gaming is more emotionally fulfilling than other hobbies; 75% feel they do something meaningful when they play.

Bain's 2025 report makes the hierarchy explicit: customization, social connection, and creativity now rank higher than visual fidelity. Players who prefer platform-style games rank customization and playing with friends as highly as gameplay itself; graphics are secondary.

The Xbox survey adds an emotional dimension. Six in ten players made lifelong friends through gaming. More than half say gaming communities have supported them through difficult periods. 71% hope to pass their love of gaming to the next generation.

Sources:

  • Bain & Company Gaming Report 2025: "Great Gameplay Is No Longer Enough"
  • Newzoo Global Gamer Study 2024
  • Xbox Culture of Play Report (Nov 2025)

2. Players Want Meaningful Choice and Consequences

Baldur's Gate 3 is the proof point. Larian Studios shipped a narrative RPG with zero microtransactions and deep branching choices. The market responded:

Metric Figure Source
Total copies sold 20M+ (as of late 2025) Larian Studios / VGChartz
Microtransactions Zero Larian Studios
Post-launch player growth Daily active users up 20% in 2024 vs. 2023 Michael Douse (Larian publishing director)
Steam Deck daily users +61% year over year Larian Studios

That a single-player RPG grew its active player base 20% after launch is extraordinary. The typical single-player title loses 80%+ of players within weeks. Larian attributed sustained engagement partly to official modding support (Patch 7), but the foundation was narrative depth that rewarded replaying with meaningfully different outcomes.

Customization and self-expression matter. Bain's 2025 survey found that elements resonating most with players include characters, world, story, and opportunities for self-expression. Players who prefer platform-style games rank customization as highly as gameplay.

Players detect and punish "illusion of choice." Community discussions consistently show that players share information about whether choices have real consequences. Games that offer branching dialogue with identical outcomes face backlash and lose trust.

The research framework: Self-Determination Theory (SDT). The most robust psychological model for game engagement comes from Ryan, Rigby, and Przybylski (2006). SDT holds that three basic needs predict engagement:

  1. Autonomy (meaningful choices, player agency)
  2. Competence (mastery, skill growth)
  3. Relatedness (social connection, belonging)

All three needs independently predict game enjoyment, immersion, and future play intention. SDT outperforms genre or graphics quality as a predictor of sustained engagement. Gamification designs satisfying these needs promote deeper, longer lasting engagement.

  • BG3 Daily Active Users +20% (GameRant)

3. Players Hate Repetitive, Static NPCs

NPC quality is one of the most consistent complaints across RPG and open-world games. The problem is well documented in both survey data and player communities.

Source Sample Finding
Inworld AI / Bryter N=1,002; US gamers aged 16-50 99% say AI NPCs would improve gameplay; 78% would play more; 79% more likely to buy; 81% willing to pay more
Inworld AI / Bryter Same survey 52% dislike repetitive NPC dialogue; 76% want better situational awareness in NPCs; 68% want more advanced conversations

Caveat: The Inworld/Bryter survey (Oct/Nov 2022) was commissioned by Inworld AI, a company selling AI NPC technology. The 99% figure is unusually high and likely reflects question framing ("would advanced AI NPCs positively impact gameplay?" rather than "do you want AI in games?"). Treat directional, not precise.

Community evidence is abundant. Skyrim's "arrow in the knee" line became a global meme precisely because it captured the universal frustration of hearing the same NPC line hundreds of times. The Witcher 3's modding community specifically identified repetitive inter-NPC dialogue as one of the most immersion-breaking aspects of the game. Nexus Mods hosts dedicated mods to suppress repetitive NPC dialogue across multiple titles. TV Tropes catalogs the pattern under "Welcome to Corneria," with hundreds of cited examples.

What players want from NPCs:

  • NPCs that remember previous interactions
  • Dialogue that varies by context (time of day, recent events, player reputation)
  • NPCs that respond to player actions (not just scripted triggers)
  • Ambient behavior that feels purposeful, not looped

No independent (non-commissioned) large-scale survey focused exclusively on NPC expectations was found. This represents a gap in available market research. The Inworld data, despite its bias, aligns directionally with the qualitative evidence from player communities.

Sources:

  • Inworld AI / Bryter: "The Future of NPCs" Report (2022)
  • Inworld AI Press Release (PRNewswire)
  • TV Tropes: "Welcome to Corneria"

4. Players Want Worlds That Feel Alive

Source Sample Date Key Finding
Google Cloud / Harris Poll N=615 game developers; 5 countries June/July 2025 37% of devs say players want games that feel more "alive"; 34% say players expect smarter, more adaptive NPCs
Google Cloud / Harris Poll Same survey Same 89% of devs say player standards are rising due to AI; demand for responsive worlds, quality animations, NPCs that remember context
GameAnalytics Survey of players 18-34 2024 62% feel overwhelmed by open-world maps; 57% abandon after completing ~30% of content

The Google Cloud data (developer survey, not player survey) captures the supply side: developers recognize that players expect more from game worlds but lack the tools to deliver it. Only 5% of developers currently use AI for player-facing features, despite 90% using it in workflows.

The open-world market keeps growing, but players reject empty worlds. The global open-world game market is projected to grow from USD ~27B (2024) to USD ~54B by 2033-2034 at a CAGR of 8.5% (Business Research Insights). Players are not rejecting large worlds. They reject large, empty, repetitive worlds. The GameAnalytics data showing 62% feeling overwhelmed and 57% abandoning is not about map size. It is about map density and variety.

Sources:

  • Google Cloud 2025 Games Report
  • Google Cloud Press Release (Aug 2025)
  • Open World Game Market Size (Business Research Insights)
  • GameAnalytics: Open World Fatigue Discussion

5. Players Are Time-Poor and Increasingly Selective

Players have less time, buy fewer games, and finish almost none of them.

Metric Figure Source
PC gamers playing <5 hrs/week 40% MIDiA Research Q4 2024
Console gamers playing <5 hrs/week 36% MIDiA Research Q4 2024
Average game completion rate (Steam) 14% mean, 10% median (N=1,616 games) Fennewald & Kievit-Kylar, 2019 (ScienceDirect)
US players buying ≤2 games/year 63% Circana Q3 2025 (N=2,500; ages 13+)
Top 5 most-played PS5 games (2025 vs. 2024) Identical (Fortnite, CoD, GTA V, Roblox, Minecraft) Circana Player Engagement Tracker
Players feeling overwhelmed by choices ~50% Xbox Culture of Play 2025 (N=1,500)
Players finding curation useful ~90% Xbox Culture of Play 2025

The picture is stark. A plurality of PC and console gamers play fewer than five hours per week. The average Steam game loses 86% of its players before they finish the primary content. Nearly two-thirds of US players buy two games or fewer per year. And the most-played games in 2025 are exactly the same titles as 2024.

This means new games compete against entrenched incumbents for a shrinking share of player time and wallet. Players are not expanding their portfolios. They are retreating to known quantities. The Xbox data confirms this: roughly half of players feel overwhelmed by the number of choices available, and nine in ten want better curation.

The implication for studios: every hour of content that players skip is wasted production cost. Adaptive systems that tailor pacing, difficulty, and narrative to individual players can increase completion rates and lifetime engagement.

6. Generational Shift: Social, Immersive, Expressive

Younger generations treat games as social platforms, identity spaces, and primary entertainment.

Metric Figure Source
Gen Z: gaming as primary social space 58% Multiple sources (Deloitte, industry aggregates)
Gen Z: met people through games they otherwise wouldn't have 70% ESA 2025 Essential Facts (N=5,000+)
Gen Z: believe games can introduce new friends 89% ESA 2025 Essential Facts
Gen Z: met a good friend or significant other through games 63% ESA 2025 Essential Facts
Gen Alpha: play video games weekly 83% ESA 2025 Essential Facts
Gen Alpha: most active console gamers 69% (vs. 65% Gen Z) ESA 2025 Essential Facts
Under-18: list immersive platform as top game ~50% Bain 2024 Gaming Report
Young players (2-18): share of entertainment time in games 30% Bain 2024 Gaming Report
Gen Z: play mobile games 86% Tapjoy 2021 (N=1,667; US; ages 18-24)

Gaming is the social layer for Gen Z. 58% of Gen Z gamers consider gaming their primary social engagement, ahead of messaging or social media. The ESA 2025 data reinforces this: 89% of Gen Z believe games introduce them to new friends; 63% have met a close friend or significant other through gaming.

Immersive platforms dominate youth engagement. Bain's 2024 report found that nearly half of gamers under 18 list an immersive platform (Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft) as their top game. Immersive gamers spend about 1.5 hours more per week in-game than non-immersive gamers, with roughly one-third of that time spent on activities other than gameplay: socializing, creating, and shopping.

Gen Alpha is the most active generation of console gamers. At 69%, Gen Alpha surpasses Gen Z (65%) in console gaming participation. Their genre preferences skew toward arcade (67%), puzzles (66%), and action (62%).

7. Trust, Polish, and Fairness Are Table Stakes

Players now punish broken launches, predatory monetization, and abandoned products. Trust has become a competitive differentiator.

Metric Figure Source
Top frustration: bugs/glitches 47% Plarium/Centiment 2025 (N=1,050; US)
Top frustration: in-game ads 46% Plarium/Centiment 2025
Top frustration: server lag 34% Plarium/Centiment 2025
Gamers who avoid devs who shipped buggy games 74% Sauce Labs 2023 (N=500 gamers; US)
Devs saying pressure to ship unfinished increased 79% Sauce Labs 2023 (N=150 devs; US)
Stop Killing Games: verified EU signatures 1,294,188 (closed July 31, 2025) European Citizens' Initiative
2025 live service launches losing 80-99% of players 15+ of 19 major titles The Gamer analysis (2025)
Price hikes in H1 2025 8 (consoles, accessories, subscriptions) Gamefile

Technical quality is non-negotiable. Plarium's 2025 survey found bugs (47%) and ads (46%) frustrate players more than microtransactions (15%). Sauce Labs found that 74% of gamers actively avoid developers who previously shipped a buggy game. Meanwhile, 79% of developers say pressure to release unfinished games has increased over the past five years.

Live service games are collapsing at record rates. An analysis of 19 major live-service or live-service-adjacent titles launched in 2025 found that more than half lost over 90% of their players. Supervive lost 99% and was cancelled within five months. FragPunk retained just 2%. Highguard turned 2 million initial players into a 90% loss within one week and shut down 50 days after launch.

Players are organizing against product abandonment. The Stop Killing Games European Citizens' Initiative closed with 1,294,188 verified signatures across 24 EU member states, exceeding the 1 million threshold. The European Commission must respond by July 2026. This represents an unprecedented level of organized consumer action in gaming.

Prices keep rising. Nintendo, Sony, and Microsoft collectively issued 8 price hikes in the first six months of 2025 across consoles, accessories, and subscription services. Nintendo launched Mario Kart World at $80. Microsoft raised Game Pass Ultimate from $20/month to $30/month. Industry analysts expect GTA VI to launch at up to $100.

8. The AI Paradox

Players want the outcomes AI can deliver (smarter NPCs, adaptive worlds, personalized pacing) but increasingly reject the label "AI" in games. This creates a positioning challenge and an opportunity.

Source Sample Date Key Finding
Quantic Foundry N=1,799 Oct-Dec 2025 85% below-neutral on Gen AI in games; 63% chose the most negative option
Quantic Foundry Earlier survey 2024 Q2-Q4 "AI-generated quests/dialogue" was 46% negative; same items now 77-83% negative
Quantic Foundry Comparison 2024 Blockchain games: 79% negative. AI is now more disliked than blockchain.
MIDiA Research N=6,300 Late 2024 Majority of gamers (including daily players) were neutral; AI rarely impacted purchasing decisions
GDC 2026 N=2,300+ devs Jan 2026 52% say Gen AI has negative impact on industry (up from 30% in 2025, 18% in 2024)
GDC 2026 Same survey Same Only 7% say positive impact (down from 13% in 2025)
Google Cloud 2025 N=615 devs Mid-2025 90% use AI in workflows; only 5% for player-facing features

Sentiment collapsed in one year. Quantic Foundry's Nick Yee noted that such a highly-skewed negative response (85% below neutral, 63% at the floor) is rare in their years of survey research. The shift from 46% negative to 77-83% negative on AI-generated quests/dialogue happened in roughly 12 months. AI in games is now viewed more negatively than blockchain was at its peak backlash (79% negative).

The MIDiA survey captured the "before." In late 2024, the majority of gamers were neutral about developers using Gen AI, and AI rarely impacted purchasing decisions. One year later, sentiment had cratered. This speed of shift is unusual and likely reflects a combination of high-profile AI failures, labor concerns, and cultural backlash.

Developers are turning against AI too. The GDC 2026 report shows 52% of developers now believe Gen AI is harming the industry, up from just 18% two years prior. Workers in visual art (64%), game design/narrative (63%), and programming (59%) hold the most unfavorable views. Only executives and business operations staff remain net positive (19%).

The usage gap tells the real story. 90% of developers use AI in their workflows. Only 5% deploy it in player-facing features. This means the industry treats AI as a production tool, not a product feature. The reason: player backlash risk.

Hybrid design is the path forward. Players want the outcomes AI enables: NPCs that remember, worlds that react, stories that adapt. They reject experiences that feel generated, generic, or that displace human creative labor. The winning formula is authored + AI improvisation: human designers create the structure, tone, and boundaries; AI fills in the adaptive layer players cannot distinguish from hand-crafted content.
