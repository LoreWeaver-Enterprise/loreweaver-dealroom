2. Architect (live SaaS product)

2.1What is Architect?

A live B2B SaaS authoring tool that converts unstructured narrative inputs (design docs, world bibles, lore wikis) into engine-ready structured data: Unreal DataTables, Unity ScriptableObjects, and JSON. 15 AI operations from lore upload to export.

2.2What is the demonstrated cost reduction?

99.8% versus manual structuring. The comparison is hourly narrative-designer cost to produce the same engine-ready dataset by hand, measured against Architect's token-pass cost at our 4x markup.

2.3How many users does Architect have?

30 active users as of March 2026, all individually onboarded. EUR 100 in first booked revenue in April 2026 (Stripe). Pricing is usage-based on AI tokens at a 4x markup; users pay only for what they consume.

2.4Is it really live?

Yes. Production app at the URL shown in the deal room. Stripe integration is live. The Architect demo page walks through the 15 AI operations against a real project.

2.5What does an Architect customer pay per month on average?

Average revenue per user in the first cohort is small (single digits to low double-digits in EUR) because most users are still in evaluation. The model assumes that converted studios scale token consumption sharply once a project enters production.

2.6Why is Architect free at the entry tier?

We strip procurement friction. A narrative designer can sign up, paste in a world bible, and see results inside ten minutes without raising a PO. We monetize when usage scales, the same pattern as AWS, Twilio, and Vercel.

2.7What stops someone from building this themselves with ChatGPT and prompts?

You can produce a few good entities by hand-prompting. You cannot produce a self-consistent 1,500-entity lore graph with referential integrity, dialogue trees that respect character voice, and engine-ready typed exports. The 15-operation pipeline (extraction, normalization, relationship graph, schema mapping, validation, export) is the product.

2.8What is the technical moat for Architect?

The structured-data pipeline and the validation layer. We do not bet on prompt-engineering tricks; we bet on the only AI authoring tool that produces typed output ready to drop into a shipping engine.

3. Director (on-device runtime AI)

3.1What is Director?

An on-device runtime AI engine. It generates live NPC behavior, dynamic plot, and adaptive dialogue while a game is running, on the player's hardware, with no cloud inference cost to the studio.

3.2Is Director shipping?

Not in a commercial title yet. Director is pre-revenue. We have 8 signed LOIs from game studios, multiple active pilots, and a TRL 4 prototype with a 94% valid response rate across 100 RPG scenarios.

3.3Why on-device and not cloud?

Three reasons. (1) Per-player cloud inference is a margin killer for studios; a free-to-play game with one million daily users cannot afford a per-prompt OpenAI bill. (2) Latency: sub-three-second response on-device beats any round trip to a hyperscaler. (3) Data privacy: nothing leaves the device, which removes a class of compliance problems in EU and UK markets.

3.4What models does Director run?

Optimized small language models (SLMs) selected per platform tier. The runtime is model-agnostic by design; we ship the orchestration, memory, validation, and steering layer on top.

3.5How is Director priced?

1% royalty on revenue above EUR 100,000 per shipped title. Free during development, free below the threshold, royalty kicks in only once the studio is making real money. Compare to Unreal Engine's 5% rate (above USD 1M lifetime).

3.6Why 1% above EUR 100K and not a flat license?

A flat license up-front gates indie adoption, which is exactly the market that drives organic distribution. A pure-royalty model aligns our revenue with the studio's commercial outcome. The EUR 100K floor protects small indies entirely; the 1% rate is small enough that no CFO blocks the integration on cost grounds.

3.7Who validates the on-device thesis?

NVIDIA ACE is the loudest external validation: NVIDIA is investing significantly in on-device narrative AI, but it is RTX-only infrastructure and ships no creative tools. Director sits on top of that direction of travel as the creative and orchestration layer.

3.8What is the technical risk on Director?

Two things: (a) on-device SLM quality continues to improve, but generation quality at low parameter counts remains the binding constraint; (b) engine integration work (Unreal, Unity) is real engineering effort per title. We mitigate (a) by being model-agnostic and improving the orchestration layer faster than model quality plateaus; we mitigate (b) by building reusable Unreal and Unity middleware shipped as one SDK.

4. Market and competition

4.1How big is the market?

Combined TAM 2030 (low case): EUR 5.0B. Architect TAM 2025: EUR 2.6B. Director TAM 2035 (gaming plus beyond-gaming, low case): EUR 1.4B. Beyond-gaming applications (digital twins, AV and robotics simulation, military training, film and TV, training) push the long-range TAM materially higher; we keep the headline number conservative.

4.2Who are the closest competitors?

articy:draft X is the 15-year incumbent for authoring; it added bolt-on AI extensions but has no structured-data pipeline and no AI-powered runtime. Inworld AI raised about USD 125M, depended on cloud inference, and pivoted away from gaming after the unit economics did not work. Convai operates in a similar cloud-NPC space and faces the same headwind. Twine and Ink are open-source authoring tools that pre-date AI.

4.3What is the unique position?

LoreWeaver is the only stack with both an integrated authoring layer (Architect) and an on-device runtime layer (Director). No competitor covers both. articy authors but does not run AI at runtime; Inworld and Convai run AI at runtime but cannot scale economically and do not own authoring.

4.4The "world models" companies are raising billions. Are you competing with them?

No: complementary. Runway, Luma, World Labs, Decart, and AMI Labs collectively are around USD 20.9B in combined valuation building world generation. They produce environments. None of them produce stories, persistent characters, or quest structures beyond about 60 seconds. Director is the narrative layer that sits on top of those models. They are top-of-funnel for us, not competition.

4.5Why has nobody built this before?

Three pre-conditions had to align: (1) inference cost drops of about 90% in two years, (2) small language models good enough to run on consumer hardware, and (3) studios admitting that hand-authored narrative does not scale to modern content expectations. All three only converged in the past 24 months.

4.6What stops Epic, Unity, or NVIDIA from copying you?

Epic and Unity are engine companies; their incentive is to maintain platform neutrality, not to ship narrative AI. NVIDIA validated the direction with ACE but explicitly does not ship creative tools. The market gap is intentional on their part. If Unity ships a competing layer, our middleware still runs on Unreal (and vice versa); our value is portable across engines, theirs is not.

4.7What is the "narrative infrastructure layer" framing?

We position LoreWeaver as the layer between game engines and AI inference: the same way Stripe sits between commerce and payment rails, or Twilio sits between applications and telecom. Studios build games; LoreWeaver provides the narrative pipes.

5. Business model and pricing

5.1What is the revenue mix between Architect and Director?

Architect dominates revenue in years 1 to 2 (the wedge), Director becomes the larger line by year 4 as the first royalty-bearing titles ship.

5.2What is Architect's gross margin?

Gross margin scales with the 4x markup on AI tokens. At full scale, blended gross margin sits in the high 70% to mid 80% range, in line with typical usage-based AI SaaS.

5.3What is Director's gross margin?

On-device runtime carries no per-player cloud cost, so Director gross margin is structurally higher than any cloud-NPC competitor: above 90% once shipped. The cost base is engineering and integration, not inference.

5.4What is the CAC profile?

Architect customer acquisition is content-led and developer-led (community, demos, integrations). Director customer acquisition is enterprise sales into studios with a 6-to-18 month cycle; LOI to revenue is the relevant funnel metric, and we expect a meaningful share of the 8 current LOIs to convert.

5.5What does the model assume for net retention?

Architect: usage expansion drives net retention above 100% as customer projects scale from prototype to production. Director: per-title royalty creates a "land and expand" pattern across a studio's catalog.

5.6Are you a SaaS or a royalty business?

Both, intentionally. Architect is usage-based SaaS and provides predictable monthly revenue from many small customers; Director is royalty-based and provides large lumpy revenue from a few shipped titles. Together they smooth the curve.

10. Legal and corporate structure

10.1What is the legal entity?

Loreweaver Holding B.V., a Dutch besloten vennootschap, statutory seat Amsterdam, Noord-Holland. Incorporation is in flight with the notary at the time of this document.

10.2What is the planned post-round structure?

A three-BV structure: Loreweaver Holding B.V. owns the operating entity and IP entity; founders hold their personal stakes through a STAK (stichting administratiekantoor) for clean voting and economic-rights separation. The STAK pattern is standard practice for Dutch tech companies and simplifies later financings.

10.3Why STAK?

STAK keeps administrative trading of certificates (economic rights) clean while consolidating voting in the foundation. Practically: simpler future rounds, simpler ESOP administration, simpler exit mechanics.

10.4Who is the notary?

Engaged and in flight. The notary deed for incorporation and the holding structure is being executed in parallel with this round. We will share the notary's identity and the draft deed under NDA on request.

10.5What IP is held by the entity?

All Architect and Director source code, models, and trademarks. Founder IP assignments are signed.

10.6Are there any outstanding liabilities or claims?

None known. No litigation, no employment disputes, no IP claims against the company.

10.7What is the tax structure?

Dutch holding plus operating BV is the most tax-efficient structure for a NL-based startup with international customers; the innovatiebox regime is available once we qualify, which materially lowers the effective tax rate on IP-derived profits.

12. Risks and mitigations

12.1What are the largest risks?

(1) Director is pre-revenue and depends on game-studio sales cycles. (2) On-device SLMs may plateau in quality before the use case is fully covered. (3) Founder concentration: two people own most of the critical knowledge. (4) Architect ARPU is unproven at production scale. (5) Competitive entry from an incumbent engine vendor or a well-funded model lab.

12.2How do you mitigate game-studio sales-cycle risk?

Architect produces revenue in months, not years; it carries the company while Director pilots mature. We do not need a Director royalty to hit Seed-stage milestones.

12.3How do you mitigate on-device LLM constraints?

Model-agnostic runtime. We do not bet on a single model; we bet on the orchestration, memory, and validation layer that wraps any sufficiently capable SLM. As models improve, our product improves; as quantization improves, our addressable hardware floor drops.

12.4How do you mitigate founder concentration?

Documentation discipline (architecture, decisions, code), backup advisors, and a hiring plan that explicitly de-risks both critical paths within 12 months of round close.

12.5What if Unity or Epic ships a competing layer?

Our middleware ships against both engines. Engine-vendor moves typically lock to one ecosystem and ship slow. The first credible competitor by an incumbent is a positive market signal as well as a threat.

12.6What if Architect's 4x markup compresses?

Pricing power follows differentiation. As long as Architect produces typed, engine-ready output and competitors produce free text, the markup holds. If markups compress in the long run, we offset with higher token volume and Director royalties.

12.7What if a model lab moves into authoring or runtime?

Model labs do not ship game-engine middleware or signed studio integrations. The distance between a research model and a shipped Unreal SDK is the distance we own. If a model lab acquires that capability, it is a likely buyer.

12.8What about regulatory risk?

EU AI Act compliance applies; on-device inference removes a significant share of the data-processing surface, which simplifies compliance materially compared to a cloud-NPC competitor.
