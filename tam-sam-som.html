3. Architect (Designer) TAM / SAM / SOM

3.1 TAM Methodology

Architect TAM = Global Game Revenue x Narrative Spend %

Narrative design encompasses story writing, dialogue systems, branching logic, quest design, localization of narrative content, and associated tooling. Industry cost breakdowns place design at 10% to 15% of total development budgets; narrative/writing is a subset at approximately 1% to 6% of total game revenue, depending on genre mix.

Note on the 1-6% range: This is a modeled estimate derived from the bottom-up Narrative Cost Model. That model calculates €38,679 per gameplay hour (first language, fully loaded) across 15 production activities, scaling to €184,143 per hour at 12 languages. Applied to approximately 170,000 gameplay hours produced annually, total industry narrative spend is estimated at €5.95B to €8.37B, representing approximately 3.4% to 4.8% of the €175B software revenue baseline. No single published source provides a definitive narrative-as-percentage-of-revenue figure; the 1-6% range reflects variation across genres (visual novels and RPGs at the high end, action and sports titles at the low end).

5. Director Beyond Gaming: World Generation Models

5.1 The Opportunity

AI world generation models create interactive 3D environments from text, images, or video in real time. These models generate geometry, lighting, and physics, but they produce empty worlds with no narrative logic, quest systems, or NPC behavior. Director fills that gap as narrative middleware.

This market extends far beyond gaming. Every industry that builds interactive 3D environments needs coherent scenarios, adaptive dialogue, and procedural storytelling. Director's on-device runtime applies directly to these use cases.

Private capital flowing into world generation models has accelerated rapidly. Combined valuations of the four largest pure-play world model startups exceed $16B as of March 2026.

5.6 Why This Matters for Director

  1. Every world model company generates environments without narrative. Decart, World Labs, DeepMind, and Runway produce geometry and visuals. None generate quests, NPC behavior trees, or adaptive dialogue. Director plugs directly into this gap.
  2. The investment signal is massive. Over $3B raised by world model startups in the last 12 months alone. These companies will need narrative middleware before their products ship.
  3. On-device is essential outside gaming too. Military simulations cannot send data to cloud APIs. Training environments require deterministic local inference. Director's on-device architecture serves these constraints by design.
  4. Gaming is the beachhead, not the ceiling. Director proves itself in games first, then expands to film, training, and simulation using the same runtime with vertical-specific content templates.
